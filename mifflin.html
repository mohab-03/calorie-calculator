Mifflin-St Jeor Equation

The Mifflin-St Jeor equation is the most accurate formula for calculating your BMR. Developed in 1990, it's the gold standard used by health professionals worldwide.

The Equation

For men: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) − (5 × age in years) + 5

For women: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) − (5 × age in years) − 161

Key Facts

  • Developed by: Mark D. Mifflin and Susan T. St Jeor at the University of Florida
  • Year: 1990
  • Purpose: To improve upon the outdated Harris-Benedict equation (1919)
  • Accuracy: Most accurate predictive equation for BMR, recommended by the American Dietetic Association
  • Based on: Weight, height, age, and gender

Why It Matters

This equation provides the foundation for calculating your daily calorie needs. Your BMR is multiplied by an activity factor to determine your TDEE, which is essential for weight management, whether you're trying to lose fat, maintain weight, or build muscle.
